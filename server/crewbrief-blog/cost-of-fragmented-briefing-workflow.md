# The Cost of a Fragmented Briefing Workflow: A Calculator for Part 135 Operators

**By CrewBrief Operations** · May 2026 · 5 min read

---

Every operator knows their fuel burn per hour. Most know their maintenance cost per cycle. Almost none know their briefing cost per shift.

The cost of manual, fragmented briefing workflows is hidden — it doesn't appear on a P&L statement or a maintenance invoice. But it is real, measurable, and significant.

## The Calculation

The cost of manual briefings has three components: crew time, missed-item risk, and compliance overhead.

### Component 1: Crew Time

The formula is straightforward:

```
Crew hourly rate × Minutes wasted per shift ÷ 60 × Crews per day × Operating days per year
```

**Parameters:**
- Average crew hourly rate (fully loaded): $60–$120/hr (captain + FO combined)
- Minutes wasted per shift gathering briefings: 15–30 minutes
- Crews per day: varies by fleet size and schedule
- Operating days per year: 250–365 depending on utilization

**Example — 20-aircraft charter operator:**
- Crew rate: $80/hr (combined)
- Waste: 20 min/shift
- Crews: 40/day (2 crews per aircraft, single shift)
- Operating days: 300/year

**Annual cost: $80 × (20÷60) × 40 × 300 = $320,000**

That is $320,000 per year in hidden crew-time cost that produces zero safety value.

### Component 2: Missed-Item Risk

Calculating the cost of missed NOTAMs, stale weather data, and inconsistent FRAT scoring is more difficult — but the potential liability is orders of magnitude larger than crew time.

Consider a single missed runway closure NOTAM:
- Diversion fuel: $2,000–$5,000
- Passenger disruption: $5,000–$20,000 (rebooking, hotels, compensation)
- Schedule disruption: $10,000–$50,000 (downstream cancellations)
- Regulatory exposure: Priceless (FAA enforcement, SMS findings)

Even one such event per year can exceed the total crew-time cost.

### Component 3: Compliance Overhead

Manual audit preparation — collecting briefing records, verifying FRAT completion, documenting the standard of care — consumes ops staff time that could be spent on value-added work.

A Part 135 operator with 10 aircraft typically spends 5–10 hours per month on briefing-related compliance tasks. At $50/hr staff cost, that is $3,000–$6,000 per year.
