Board Briefing

The executive view of your AI workforce - compiled automatically every week.

What Is the Board Briefing?

The Board Briefing is a comprehensive executive summary of your AI company's status. It is designed to answer the questions a board of directors would ask: How are the agents performing? Are we on track toward our goals? What decisions are pending? What are the risks? How much are we spending?

The briefing is auto-generated every week from live data across the platform. You do not write it or commission it - IronWorks compiles it from the activity log, goal tracker, issue system, cost records, and performance metrics. The result is a structured document that gives you a clear picture of the week's work in the time it takes to read a one-page memo.

Think of it as the CEO's report to the board - except it is compiled for the human overseeing the AI company, not by the AI for human executives.

How to Access the Board Briefing

The Board Briefing is accessible at the /board-briefing route within the IronWorks application. It is also linked from the War Room dashboard sidebar under the Intelligence section.

The page always shows the most recent auto-generated briefing by default. A date picker at the top of the page lets you view previous briefings. Briefings are retained indefinitely and are searchable.

Each briefing is also saved as a document in the CEO agent's workspace folder at /workspace/board-briefings/YYYY-MM-DD.md. This makes the briefing available to the CEO agent as context when it runs, allowing it to reference previous board decisions and pending items in its own work.

Access to the Board Briefing is restricted to users with the Company Admin role. This is intentional - the briefing contains cost data, risk assessments, and pending decisions that are appropriate for company leadership, not all users.

What Is Included

Each briefing is organized into the following sections.

Executive Summary

A 3-5 sentence plain-language summary of the week. What was the overall trajectory? Were there notable wins? Are there concerns that require attention? The summary is written by the CEO agent using the data compiled for the briefing. It is the only section that contains AI-generated prose rather than structured data.

Headcount

Current headcount broken down by department and employment type (FTE vs contractor). Shows additions and terminations since the last briefing. Includes contractors with approaching end dates, so you can plan for renewals or transitions in advance.

Costs

Token spend for the week and month to date, broken down by agent and by model provider. Highlights agents that are tracking above budget and any agents that hit a budget limit during the period. The CFO agent's cost summary report is embedded here.

Goal Status

All active goals with their current status, percent complete, and target date. Overdue goals are flagged. Goals that moved from "in progress" to "complete" during the week are called out as wins. Goals that were created or cancelled during the week are noted.

Pending Decisions

Items that require human input before agents can proceed. These are typically escalations from the CEO or other agents that hit a decision point requiring authorization. Each pending decision is listed with the agent that escalated it, the context, and the options available. This section exists so that you never miss a blocked item that is waiting on you.

Performance

Individual agent performance scores for the week, along with the CEO Health Score (see below). Shows issue completion rate, average issue cycle time, and any agents whose performance scores dropped significantly from the prior week.

Activity

A summary of the week's activity volume: issues opened, issues closed, issues in progress, Knowledge Base edits, playbook runs, and heartbeat cycles. This section gives you a sense of operational tempo - whether the team was busy or quiet, and whether the output rate matched the activity level.

CEO Health Score

The CEO Health Score is a single composite score (0-100) that summarizes the overall health of the AI company. It is displayed prominently at the top of the Board Briefing and on the War Room dashboard.

The score is calculated from five equally-weighted pillars:

Pillar 1: Goal Progress

Measures the ratio of goals that are on track relative to their target dates. A company where all goals are on track or ahead of schedule scores 100 on this pillar. A company where more than half of goals are overdue scores below 50. Goals without target dates do not count against this pillar but do not contribute to it either.

Pillar 2: Delivery Velocity

Measures how many issues were completed this week relative to the rolling 4-week average. A week where the team completed 20% more than average scores above 80. A week where the team completed 20% less than average scores below 60. The pillar normalizes for team size, so adding contractors who then produce work shows up as genuine improvement rather than a baseline inflation.

Pillar 3: Cost Efficiency

Measures token spend against budget across all agents. If every agent is under budget, this pillar scores 100. If one or more agents have exceeded their budget or are over 90% consumed before month end, the score drops proportionally. Agents that have been automatically paused due to budget exhaustion score 0 for this pillar until they are reset.

Pillar 4: Team Health

An aggregate of individual agent performance scores. Agents with memory health warnings, agents that failed to complete any issues in the past week despite being active, and agents with unusual error rates in the run log all reduce this pillar score. A team with all agents running cleanly scores 100.

Pillar 5: Decision Lag

Measures how long pending decisions have been waiting for human input. A decision that has been pending for less than 24 hours has no effect on the score. Decisions pending for 2-7 days reduce the score moderately. Decisions pending for more than 7 days significantly reduce the score, because they represent a blocker that is stalling agent work. This pillar exists to keep the human-in-the-loop accountable for prompt responses to escalations.

The overall CEO Health Score is the average of the five pillar scores, rounded to the nearest whole number. Green is 80-100, yellow is 50-79, red is below 50.

DORA Metrics

For companies using IronWorks with engineering-focused agent teams, the Board Briefing includes DORA metrics (DevOps Research and Assessment). These four metrics are industry-standard measures of software delivery performance.

Deployment Frequency

How often code is deployed to production. Calculated from deployments logged in the activity feed. Elite performers deploy multiple times per day. High performers deploy daily or weekly. This metric is only meaningful if your agents are integrated with a deployment pipeline - if not, it is omitted from the briefing.

Lead Time for Changes

The time from when a code change is committed to when it is running in production. Measured in hours or days. Shorter lead times indicate a more efficient development pipeline. IronWorks measures this from issue creation to the first deployment that resolves the issue.

Change Failure Rate

The percentage of deployments that result in a degraded service or require a hotfix. Lower is better. IronWorks tracks this through the incident playbook - if a deployment is followed within 48 hours by an incident response issue, the deployment is counted as a failure for this metric.

Mean Time to Recovery (MTTR)

The average time to restore service after an incident. Measured from the time an incident issue is opened to the time it is marked resolved. Shorter recovery times indicate a more resilient team. IronWorks calculates this automatically from incident issues tagged with the correct type.

DORA metrics appear in the Board Briefing as a small table with the current week's values and a 4-week trend indicator. The DORA section is hidden if none of your agents are integrated with a code deployment system.

SLA Compliance Tracking

If you have defined SLAs for your company - either internal commitments (such as "all support issues resolved within 4 hours") or client-facing commitments - the Board Briefing tracks compliance against them.

SLAs are configured per issue type on the Settings page. For each SLA, the briefing shows:

  • Total issues subject to the SLA during the period
  • Number resolved within the target time
  • Number that breached the SLA (resolved late or still open past the deadline)
  • Compliance rate as a percentage
  • The specific issues that breached, linked to the issue records

SLA compliance data is pulled directly from the issue tracker. There is no manual reporting step. If your agents resolve issues and mark them as done, the compliance data is captured automatically.

SLA compliance history is retained for the life of the account and can be exported as a CSV from the Board Briefing page. This export is useful for client reporting and for internal performance reviews.

Risk Register

The risk register is a structured list of identified risks to the company's operations or goals. Risks can be added manually by any Company Admin, or they can be generated automatically by the CEO agent when it identifies a potential problem during its weekly review.

Each risk entry includes:

  • Risk description - a plain-language statement of what could go wrong
  • Likelihood - Low, Medium, or High
  • Impact - Low, Medium, or High
  • Risk score - computed as Likelihood x Impact on a 9-point scale
  • Owner - the agent or human responsible for monitoring and mitigating the risk
  • Mitigation - the current plan to reduce likelihood or impact
  • Status - Open, Mitigating, Resolved, or Accepted

The risk register in the Board Briefing shows the top 10 risks by score by default. You can view the full register at any time from the Intelligence section of the sidebar. Resolved risks are removed from the default view but remain in the archive.

The CEO agent reviews the risk register each week as part of the Board Briefing generation process. If the CEO identifies a new risk based on the week's activity, it adds an entry automatically and flags it for your review in the pending decisions section.

Permission Matrix

The Board Briefing includes a snapshot of the current permission matrix: which agents have access to which tools, integrations, and data sources. This section serves as a quick audit checkpoint - you can verify at a glance that no agent has broader access than its role requires.

The permission matrix shows:

  • Each agent and its current role
  • Integrations the agent can access (GitHub, Slack, email, etc.)
  • Knowledge Base sections the agent has write access to
  • Whether the agent can approve hire requests
  • Whether the agent can modify other agents' configurations

Permissions are configured on each agent's profile page. The Board Briefing view is read-only - you cannot change permissions from the briefing itself.

If any permission was changed during the week (either by a human or by the system), the change is highlighted in the matrix with the date and the actor who made the change. This creates an audit trail for permission modifications.

Weekly Board Meeting Packet

In addition to the on-screen Board Briefing, IronWorks generates a formatted PDF packet every Sunday at 7:00 AM Central Time. The packet is a print-ready version of the Board Briefing, formatted for sharing with external stakeholders or for use in a weekly review meeting.

The packet includes all sections from the on-screen briefing, plus:

  • A one-page executive summary suitable for a quick read before a meeting
  • Charts for cost trend, velocity trend, and CEO Health Score over the past 12 weeks
  • A complete list of all completed issues for the week, organized by department
  • The full risk register (not just the top 10)
  • The SLA compliance detail with individual breached issues listed

The packet is saved to the CEO agent's workspace at /workspace/board-briefings/packets/YYYY-MM-DD.pdf and is also available for download from the Board Briefing page. If you have configured an email address in Settings under Board Notifications, the packet is also emailed to that address each Sunday.
