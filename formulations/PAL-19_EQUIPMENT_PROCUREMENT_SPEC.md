## 7. Recommended Procurement Strategy

### Immediate actions (Week 1)
1. Send RFQ to MixMor, S.F. Engineering, and Ross for disperser + letdown mixer
2. Send RFQ to 3 local SS fabricators for vessels and DM tank
3. Send RFQ to NPACK and Accutek for filling machine
4. Verify available 3-phase power capacity at plant location
5. Procure QC lab instruments (Stormer, Hegman, pH meter, balance) — these are off-the-shelf with immediate delivery

### Cost-saving options
- **Option A (₹25 L):** MixMor disperser + vessels + manual fill → 6-week timeline
- **Option B (₹32 L, recommended):** MixMor disperser + letdown + NPACK filler + QC lab → 8-week timeline
- **Option C (₹38 L):** Ross disperser + NPACK filler + full QC lab → 10-week timeline (premium quality)

**Recommendation:** Option B — best balance of cost, quality, and timeline for a Phase 1 plant.

## 8. Risk Register

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Disperser delivery delay >2 weeks | Medium | High | Order from MixMor (4–6 wk lead) instead of Ross; keep S.F. Engg as backup |
| Power upgrade incomplete at plant | Medium | High | Verify before PO; start upgrade immediately in parallel |
| Filling machine underperforms | Low | Medium | Order with factory acceptance test (FAT) clause |
| Vessel fabrication quality issues | Medium | High | Specify SS304 mill cert; inspect before payment; use reputable fabricator |
| QC instruments calibration delay | Low | Low | Order from ISO 17025 accredited supplier with calibration cert included |
| Budget overrun >15% | Medium | Medium | 10% contingency already built in; Option A as fallback |
