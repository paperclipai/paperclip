# Finished Product QC Protocol

**Document code:** PAL-21-QC-003
**Date:** 2026-05-28
**Author:** CTO
**Status:** Final — ready for validation

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the finished product quality control tests performed on every completed batch before release for filling. It specifies test methods, pass/fail limits, sampling procedures, and dispositions for out-of-specification results.

## 3. Detailed Test Methods

### 3.1 Viscosity — Stormer (KU)
**Reference:** ASTM D562
1. Condition sample to 25°C ± 1°C
2. Fill Stormer cup to brim, avoid air bubbles
3. Attach spindle, raise cup
4. Release brake, record KU reading after 30 sec stabilisation
5. Report as Krebs Units (KU)
**Pass:** 100–110 KU
**Note:** KU is the standard for brush/roller application viscosity. For spray application, adjust target to 85–95 KU.

### 3.2 pH
**Reference:** ISO 787-9
1. Calibrate pH meter with pH 7.0 and pH 9.2 buffers
2. Immerse electrode directly into paint sample
3. Record after reading stabilises (typically 1–2 min)
4. Rinse electrode with DM water between samples
**Pass:** 8.5–9.0

### 3.3 Fineness of Grind (Hegman)
**Reference:** IS 101
1. Place Hegman gauge on clean, level surface
2. Put a small amount of paint at the deep end
3. Draw scraper down at uniform speed
4. Read the point where first continuous scratch pattern appears
5. Record in Hegman units (0–8, where 8 = finest)
**Pass:** ≥ 6 Hegman units (≤ 25 µm)

### 3.4 Density
**Reference:** IS 101
1. Weigh clean, dry 100 mL density cup
2. Fill cup with paint, avoid air bubbles
3. Level off with spatula
4. Weigh filled cup. Density = (filled wt − empty wt) / 100
**Pass:** Per base type as specified in §2

### 3.5 Opacity (Contrast Ratio)
**Reference:** IS 101 (Part 8/Sec 1)
1. Drawdown paint film (150 µm wet film) on opacity chart (black/white)
2. Dry 24 hr at 25°C (or 4 hr at 50°C for accelerated)
3. Measure Y-reflectance on black (Yb) and white (Yw) sections
4. Contrast Ratio = Yb / Yw × 100
**Pass:** Per base type as specified in §2

### 3.6 Gloss (60°)
**Reference:** ASTM D523
1. Cast film at 150 µm wet film on glass plate
2. Dry 24 hr at 25°C
3. Calibrate gloss meter with gloss standard
4. Measure at 60° geometry; 3 readings, report average
**Pass:** <5 gloss units (matte finish)

### 3.7 Drying Time
**Reference:** IS 101
**Touch dry:** Apply paint at 150 µm on glass plate. Gently place cotton ball on surface. If cotton fibres do not stick — touch dry. Record time.
**Hard dry:** Apply paint at 150 µm on glass. Place thumb firmly on film. If no thumbprint remains — hard dry. Record time.
**Pass:** Touch dry <30 min; Hard dry <4 hr

### 3.8 Scrub Resistance
**Reference:** IS 15495
1. Apply paint at 150 µm wet film on scrub panel
2. Dry 7 days at 25°C
3. Mount on scrub tester with abrasive medium
4. Record cycles to first substrate exposure
**Pass:** >500 cycles (standard interior); >1000 (premium)

### 3.9 Heat Stability
**Reference:** In-house (accelerated aging)
1. Fill 250 mL HDPE container ¾ full
2. Seal, place in 50°C ± 2°C oven
3. Inspect weekly: viscosity, pH, odour, settling, coagulation
4. 4 weeks at 50°C ≈ 24 months at 25°C (Q10 = 2)
**Pass:** No coagulation, viscosity drift <10% of initial, pH drift <0.5 units

### 3.10 Freeze-Thaw Stability
1. Fill 100 mL HDPE container ¾ full
2. Freeze at −5°C for 16 hr
3. Thaw at 25°C for 8 hr
4. Repeat 5 cycles
5. Inspect for coagulation, viscosity change
**Pass:** No coagulation, viscosity change <20%

## 4. Sampling Plan for Final QC

| Batch Size | Sample Points | Volume | Container |
|---|---|---|---|
| < 500 L | 3 points (top, mid, bottom of mix vessel) | 1 L composite | HDPE container, labelled |
| ≥ 500 L | 5 points (plus 2 from fill line start/end) | 2 L composite | HDPE container, labelled |

**Procedure:**
- Draw samples after 15 min homogenisation mixing at letdown speed
- Fill sample containers completely (no headspace) to minimise skin formation
- Label with: Batch #, Base type, Date, Time, Operator initials
- Allow 30 min de-aeration before testing

## 5. Out-of-Specification (OOS) Disposition

| OOS Type | Severity | Disposition |
|---|---|---|
| Viscosity 95–99 KU or 111–115 KU | Minor | Adjust with thickener/water, re-test. If within spec after 1 adjustment → RELEASE |
| Viscosity <95 or >115 KU after adjustment | Major | HOLD. QC Manager review. Can blend with a compensating batch? If yes, document. If no → RECYCLE |
| pH 8.0–8.4 or 9.0–9.5 | Minor | Adjust with ammonia/water, re-test. If within spec → RELEASE |
| pH <8.0 or >9.5 | Major | HOLD. QC Manager decides: rework or reject |
| Hegman <6 | Major | HOLD. Return to grind stage. Max 1 re-grind. If still <6 → REJECT |
| Opacity < spec | Major | HOLD. Check TiO₂ level. If true deficiency → downgrade to lower base tier or REJECT |
| Scrub <500 cycles | Critical | REJECT. Not fit for interior use. Consider rework with additional binder |
| Coagulation / phase separation | Critical | REJECT immediately. No rework. Document root cause |
| Odour — strong ammonia or foul | Major | HOLD. If ammonia → aerate 24 hr. If foul → REJECT |

**Disposition codes:**
- **RELEASE** — batch OK for filling
- **HOLD** — pending QC Manager decision, max 7 days
- **REWORK** — return to process with documented corrective action
- **RECYCLE** — re-process as raw material for next batch (max 5% of next batch)
- **REJECT** — dispose or return to supplier (if raw material issue)

---

## 6. QC Hold and Release Procedure

1. Batch completed → QC operator draws sample, labels, places in QC hold area  
2. QC technician runs all required tests (see §2)  
3. Results recorded on BMR QC section (PAL-21-QC-004)  
4. Each test signed and dated by technician  
5. QC Supervisor reviews all results  
6. Pass → **Green tag** affixed to batch: "QC RELEASED" with date and signature  
7. Fail → **Red tag** affixed: "QC HOLD" with reason, date, operator  
8. Released batches proceed to filling line  
9. BMR archived in QC office (minimum 5 years)
