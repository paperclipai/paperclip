**Document code:** PAL-34-SOP-001

**Applies to:** All paint base types (White, Medium, Deep) at 500 L scale

## 1. Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure defines the step-by-step process for manufacturing 500 L commercial paint batches using the two-stage grind-letdown method. It covers equipment setup, raw material charging, dispersion, letdown, QC holds, transfer, filtration, and filling.

---

## 2. Scope

All commercial production batches (C-01 through C-05 and subsequent production runs) at 500 L scale.

## 4. Pre-Production Checks (Daily)

### 4.1 Equipment Checks
- [ ] Disperser VFD functional, RPM verifiable with tachometer
- [ ] Impeller 300 mm — no wear, bolts tight, shaft true
- [ ] Anchor agitator — RPM range verified, no wobble
- [ ] Transfer pump — primed, no leaks, check valve operational
- [ ] Bag filter housing — gasket intact, clamp tight
- [ ] Filler — clean, nozzles clear, no drips
- [ ] All vessels — clean, dry, drain valves closed
- [ ] All valves — position verified (open/closed per SOP section)

### 4.2 Instrument Calibration
- [ ] Tachometer — reading ±2% of reference
- [ ] Temperature probe — within ±1°C at 25°C and 50°C
- [ ] pH meter — calibrated with pH 7.0 and 9.2 buffers
- [ ] Stormer viscometer — calibrated with reference oil
- [ ] Weigh scale — zeroed, calibrated with 50 kg test weight
- [ ] Density cup — clean, dry, tare weight recorded

### 4.3 Raw Material Checks
- [ ] All raw materials: PAL-21-QC-001 release status = PASS
- [ ] Pre-weighed batches within ±0.5% of target
- [ ] Material lot numbers recorded
- [ ] Expiry dates verified (no expired materials)
- [ ] Emulsion: viscosity check (should be <2000 cP at 25°C)
- [ ] TiO₂: no lumps, free-flowing powder
- [ ] Water: DM water, conductivity <10 µS/cm

### 4.4 Safety Checks
- [ ] PPE available and worn by all operators
- [ ] Ventilation system turned on 10 min before start
- [ ] Spill kit present in production area
- [ ] Emergency shower/eyewash functional
- [ ] MSDS binder accessible
- [ ] Fire extinguisher in position, seal intact

## 5. Production Procedure

### 5.1 Stage 1: Grind — High-Speed Dispersion

**Target CPPs:**
- Tip speed: 22 m/s (≈1400 RPM with 300 mm impeller)
- Max temperature: <50°C
- Grind time: White/Medium ~27 min, Deep ~24 min (per Trial scale-up validation, grind exponent 0.20)

**Sequence:**

1. **Charge water:** Add DM water to grind vessel per formulation weight.
2. **Start disperser:** Ramp up to ~800 RPM (low speed to avoid splashing).
3. **Add dispersant:** Polyacrylate dispersant — mix 2 min.
4. **Add wetting agent:** Non-ionic surfactant — mix 1 min.
5. **Add defoamer (grind portion):** 0.25% of total formulation (+0.10% letdown top-up for 0.35% total) — mix 1 min.
6. **Add biocide:** BIT/MIT — mix 1 min.
7. **Ramp up:** Increase to ~1400 RPM.
8. **Add TiO₂:** Slowly, through sieve or by controlled dumping — avoid dust cloud. Add over 3–5 min.
9. **Add extenders sequentially:** CaCO₃ → Talc → Kaolin. Add each over 2–3 min.
10. **Add bentonite clay:** 0.1% — anti-settling agent. Mix 2 min.
11. **Grind:** Maintain at ~1400 RPM. Monitor temperature every 5 min. If temp >48°C, reduce RPM or pause.
12. **Hegman check at 15 min:** Drawdown on Hegman gauge. If ≥6, proceed. If <6, continue grinding, check every 5 min.
13. **Record endpoint:** Time, RPM, temperature, Hegman reading.
14. **HP-1 Hold:** QC operator checks and signs off.

**CAUTION:** Do not exceed 50°C. Higher temperatures can destabilise the dispersion and damage the disperser seals.

### 5.2 Stage 2: Transfer — Grind to Letdown

1. Ensure letdown vessel is clean, dry, and anchor agitator is off.
2. Open grind vessel bottom valve.
3. Start transfer pump. Target flow rate: 50 LPM.
4. Route grind paste through in-line 100 µm bag filter.
5. Monitor filter inlet pressure. If >2 bar, pause and replace bag.
6. When grind vessel is nearly empty, tilt vessel or use wand pickup for heel.
7. Record transfer start/end times, pump rate, filter pressure.
8. Flush grind vessel and transfer line with 5–10 L DM water. Add flush water to letdown vessel.
9. Close grind vessel bottom valve.
10. Document heel left in grind vessel (<5 L target).

### 5.3 Stage 3: Letdown — Low-Speed Mixing

**Target CPPs:**
- Anchor RPM: 200–500 RPM
- Emulsion addition: ≥10 min (stream-wise)
- Post-add homogenisation: ≥15 min
- Final temperature: <40°C

**Sequence:**

1. **Start anchor agitator:** 300–400 RPM.
2. **Add emulsion:** Slowly, in a steady stream, not dumped. Target addition time ≥10 min for 500 L.
   - If foaming occurs, pause addition, add defoamer top-up (0.05%), resume at slower rate.
3. **Add Texanol pre-blend:** Pre-mix Texanol 1:1 with DM water. Add slowly over 2–3 min.
4. **Add rheology modifier(s):**
   - HEC: Add pre-dissolved gel. Mix 5 min.
   - HASE: Add slowly, allow viscosity to build. Mix 5 min between additions.
5. **Adjust pH:** Add ammonia (25%) in small increments. Target pH 8.5–9.0. Mix 2 min between additions.
6. **Water letdown:** Add balance of DM water to reach target final weight.
7. **Homogenise:** Continue mixing at 300–500 RPM for ≥15 min.
8. **HP-2 Hold:** QC checks pH, preliminary viscosity, temperature, foam level.

**CAUTION:** Emulsion shock (sudden addition of cold emulsion) can cause coagulation. Always add slowly, stream-wise. If emulsion is <20°C, allow to warm to ambient before addition.

### 5.4 Stage 4: Final QC (HP-3)

1. After homogenisation, reduce anchor speed to 200 RPM.
2. De-aerate for 30 min (surface foam will dissipate).
3. QC operator draws 2 L composite sample (5 points: top, mid, bottom, fill start, fill end).
4. Label sample container: Batch C-__, Base type, Date, Time, Operator.
5. Run full QC panel per PAL-21-QC-003 (Section D of BMR).
6. Results reviewed and signed by QC Supervisor.

### 5.5 Stage 5: Filling

1. If QC disposition = RELEASE, proceed to filling.
2. Ensure filler hopper/header tank is clean.
3. Route paint through in-line 100 µm bag filter (second pass).
4. Set filler to target volume/weight per container size.
5. Check first container weight — adjust if >±1% deviation.
6. Run filling at steady rate. QC checks every 50th container for fill weight.
7. Label every container: Product code, batch number, date, quantity.
8. Record total filled containers and total filled volume.
9. Retain one container per 200 filled for QC archive.
10. Return unused paint to letdown vessel. If >10 L unused, store under slow agitation for next batch on same day.

## 6. In-Process Control Limits

| Parameter | Action Limit | Alarm Limit | Response |
|---|---|---|---|
| Grind temperature | 48°C | 50°C | Reduce RPM or pause. If >50°C → stop, cool, investigate |
| Hegman at 15 min | <6 | <5 | Continue grinding, extend by 5 min increments |
| Grind paste viscosity | No spec | Visually thick | Add 1–2% DM water if too thick for pump |
| Transfer filter pressure | 1.5 bar | 2.0 bar | Replace bag. If pressure rises immediately → investigate grind fineness |
| Letdown foam level | 5 mm | 10 mm | Add defoamer top-up; reduce RPM |
| Emulsion addition rate | 7 min | 5 min | Slow addition — coagulation risk |
| Final temperature | 35°C | 40°C | Cool before filling (>40°C can cause sweating in containers) |
| Fill weight deviation | ±1% | ±2% | Recalibrate filler |

## 7. Cleaning Between Batches

### 7.1 Between Different Base Types (White → Medium → Deep)

1. Drain and rinse grind vessel with DM water.
2. Run disperser at 800 RPM for 5 min with 50 L DM water.
3. Drain rinse water (collect in waste tank — do not send to drain).
4. Wipe down impeller shaft and vessel walls.
5. Repeat for letdown vessel — run anchor at 300 RPM with 50 L DM water for 5 min.
6. Rinse transfer pump and filter housing — disassemble and clean bag filter screen.
7. Inspect for残留 colour — repeat if tinted water remains.

### 7.2 Between Same Base Type (C-04 white after C-01 white)

1. Drain grind vessel heel (<5 L acceptable).
2. No water rinse needed if same base type.
3. Wipe down impeller and vessel walls.
4. Letdown vessel: drain heel, wipe walls. No water rinse.

### 7.3 End of Day

1. Full clean of all vessels, pump, filter, and filler.
2. Disassemble and clean bag filter housing.
3. Run 100 L hot water (60°C) through pump and filler loop.
4. Leave vessels dry and open (vents open).
5. Check and record equipment condition in shift log.

## 9. Troubleshooting Guide

| Problem | Likely Cause | Corrective Action |
|---|---|---|
| High grind temperature | RPM too high, cooling insufficient | Reduce RPM by 200, pause 5 min, resume at lower speed |
| Hegman not reaching ≥6 | Poor pigment wetting, insufficient time | Add 0.1% wetting agent, extend grind 5 min |
| Foam in letdown | Emulsion added too fast, defoamer level low | Reduce RPM, add 0.05% defoamer, extend mix 5 min |
| Viscosity too low (KU <100) | HASE level low, excess water | Add pre-dissolved HASE 0.1% increments; mix 5 min, re-test |
| Viscosity too high (KU >110) | HASE level high, too little water | Add DM water 0.5% increments; mix 5 min, re-test |
| pH <8.5 | Ammonia insufficient | Add ammonia 0.01% increments; mix 2 min, re-test |
| pH >9.0 | Excess ammonia | No adjustment — will drift down on storage. Accept if <9.2 |
| Filter blinding (pressure >2 bar) | Grind too coarse, bag damaged | Replace bag; re-check Hegman. If <6 → re-circulate grind |
| Settling in storage | Bentonite insufficient, CaCO₃ PSD coarse | Increase bentonite to 0.15% on next batch; check mill settings |
| Container sweating (after fill) | Fill temperature >40°C | Cool batch before filling (increase de-aeration time) |

## 10. Batch-to-Batch Reproducibility Criteria

| Parameter | Target | Acceptable Variation | Action if Exceeded | Validated (PAL-51, n=10) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Viscosity (KU) | 105 KU | ±3 KU between batches | Investigate raw material lot variation; re-calibrate weigh scales | Mean 105.10, range 2.4 KU ✅ |
| pH | 8.75 | ±0.2 between batches | Check ammonia solution strength; calibrate pH meter | Mean 8.75, range 0.07 ✅ |
| Opacity | per base spec | ±1% between batches | Check TiO₂ lot quality; verify weigh accuracy | Mean 97.58%, range 0.9% ✅ |
| Density | per base spec | ±0.02 g/mL | Check CaCO₃/TiO₂ ratios; verify weigh accuracy | Mean 1.35, range 0.01 ✅ |
| Hegman | ≥6 | ±0.5 Hegman | Check disperser RPM; verify pigment quality | Mean 7.13, range 1.0 ⚠️ Review disperser maintenance |
| Yield | 97%+ | ±1% | Check weigh scale; investigate process losses | Mean 99.18% ✅ |
