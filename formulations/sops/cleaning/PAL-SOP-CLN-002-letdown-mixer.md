# Cleaning SOP — Letdown Mixer

**Document:** PAL-SOP-CLN-002
**Issue:** [PAL-39](/PAL/issues/PAL-39)
**Revision:** 1.0
**Date:** 2026-05-28

---

## 1. Purpose

To define the cleaning procedure for the letdown mixing vessel and anchor agitator. Proper cleaning prevents emulsion build-up, bacterial growth, and cross-contamination between batches.

---

## 2. Equipment

| Item | Description |
|---|---|
| Letdown vessel | 500 L SS304, dished bottom |
| Anchor agitator | Variable speed, 200–500 RPM |

---

## 3. Materials & Tools

| Material | Quantity per clean |
|---|---|
| DM water (hot, 50–60°C) | 60–100 L |
| Neutral detergent (pH 7–8) | 250 mL |
| Isopropyl alcohol (70%) | 500 mL (weekly only) |
| Clean rags / lint-free wipes | 10 |
| Soft-bristle brush (nylon) | 1 |
| Putty knife (plastic) | 1 |
| High-pressure spray gun (optional) | 1 |

**PPE required:** Chemical-resistant gloves, safety goggles, rubber apron, steel-toe boots.

---

## 4. Procedure

### 4.1 Standard End-of-Batch Cleaning

**Step 1 — Drain and scrape**
1. Ensure agitator is OFF and locked out (LOTO).
2. Drain any residual paint from bottom valve into waste container or rework drum.
3. Scrape anchor arms and vessel walls with plastic putty knife.
4. Collect scrapings; do not wash bulk paint down drain.

**Step 2 — First rinse (emulsion removal)**
1. Add 40 L hot DM water to vessel.
2. Run agitator at 200 RPM for 5 min.
3. Drain to wastewater holding tank.
4. Check drain water — if opaque/milky, repeat rinse.

**Step 3 — Detergent wash**
1. Add 50 L hot DM water + 250 mL neutral detergent.
2. Run agitator at 300 RPM for 15 min.
3. Drain detergent solution.

**Step 4 — Final rinse**
1. Add 40 L fresh hot DM water.
2. Run agitator at 200 RPM for 5 min.
3. Drain completely.
4. Open bottom valve fully, verify free flow (no blockage).

**Step 5 — Visual and tactile inspection**
1. Inspect anchor arms, vessel walls, bottom dish, and drain valve.
2. Pass criteria: No visible residue, no slime/film, no odour.
3. Run clean hand across surface — should not feel tacky or slippery.
4. If residue found, repeat from Step 2.

### 4.2 Bacterial Growth / Biofilm Removal (As Needed)

If vessel has been idle >48 hr or shows slime/film:
1. Follow standard clean (4.1).
2. After Step 4, add 40 L DM water + 500 mL 70% isopropyl alcohol.
3. Agitate 300 RPM for 10 min.
4. Drain, rinse with 20 L fresh DM water.
5. Verify by swab test (ATP swab if available, or visual + odour).

### 4.3 Weekly Deep Clean

1. Follow standard clean (4.1).
2. Remove anchor agitator shaft seal cover (if accessible).
3. Inspect seal area for paint build-up; clean with soft brush.
4. Inspected bottom drain valve — disassemble and clean seat if fouled.
5. Reassemble, verify valve opens/closes smoothly.

---

## 5. Verification

| Check | Method | Pass Criteria |
|---|---|---|
| Visual | White glove wipe of anchor and vessel wall | No visible residue |
| Odour | Smell test (ventilated area) | No sour/rancid/ammonia smell |
| Drain water clarity | 500 mL in clear glass beaker | Clear to faint tint |
| pH of final rinse | pH strip | 6.5–8.5 |

---

## 6. Frequency

| Type | When |
|---|---|
| Standard | After every batch |
| Biofilm clean | When idle >48 hr or slime observed |
| Deep clean | Weekly |
| Bottom valve disassembly | Monthly |

---

## 7. Waste Handling

- All rinsate to process wastewater holding tank.
- Scrapings to hazardous waste drum.
- Alcohol-waste rinsate to separate labelled container for ETP (effluent treatment plant).
- Record volume in Environmental Log (PAL-ENV-001).
