# Cleaning SOP — SS304 Process Vessels (2× 500 L)

**Date:** 2026-05-28

**Applies to:**
- Vessel V-101: Grind vessel (500 L SS304)
- Vessel V-102: Letdown vessel (500 L SS304)

## 1. Purpose

To define the periodic cleaning, passivation, and inspection procedure for the two SS304 process vessels. This SOP covers non-routine cleaning (between campaigns, after maintenance, passivation) beyond the batch-to-batch cleaning covered in [PAL-SOP-CLN-001](/formulations/sops/cleaning/PAL-SOP-CLN-001-disperser.md) and [PAL-SOP-CLN-002](/formulations/sops/cleaning/PAL-SOP-CLN-002-letdown-mixer.md).

## 2. Materials & Tools

| Material | Quantity per vessel |
|---|---|

| Neutral detergent (pH 7–8) | 500 mL |
| Citric acid (food grade, anhydrous) | 2 kg |

| Ammonium bifluoride (passivation paste, optional) | As needed |

**PPE required:** Chemical-resistant gloves, goggles, face shield (for acid/alkali), rubber apron, steel-toe boots, respirator (if ammonium bifluoride used).

## 3. Procedure

### 3.1 Campaign Change Cleaning (between different base types)

Performed when switching production between significantly different formulations (e.g., White Base → Deep Base or interior → exterior).

**Step 1 — Initial rinse**
1. Ensure vessel is empty, agitator/disperser OFF, LOTO applied.
2. Fill vessel to 50% capacity with hot DM water (60–70°C).
3. Agitate at 200 RPM for 10 min.
4. Drain to wastewater.

**Step 2 — Detergent wash**
1. Fill to 50% with hot DM water + 500 mL neutral detergent.
2. Agitate at 300 RPM for 20 min.
3. Drain.

**Step 3 — Acid rinse (citric acid)**
1. Fill to 30% with hot DM water (60–70°C).
2. Add 2 kg citric acid (creates ~2% w/w solution).
3. Agitate at 100 RPM (gentle sloshing) for 30 min.
4. Drain.

**Step 4 — Alkali rinse (NaOH)**
1. Fill to 30% with hot DM water.
2. Add 2 L of 10% NaOH solution.
3. Agitate gently at 100 RPM for 15 min.
4. Drain.

**Step 5 — Final rinse**
1. Fill to 50% with fresh DM water.
2. Agitate at 200 RPM for 10 min.
3. Drain completely.
4. Verify rinse water pH is 6.5–8.5 with pH strip.
5. If pH outside range, repeat final rinse.

**Step 6 — Dry**
1. Leave vessel lid open.
2. Wipe interior dry with clean lint-free cloths.
3. Allow to air-dry for minimum 30 min before next use.

### 3.2 Quarterly Passivation

Performed every 3 months or whenever surface discolouration (rust blooms, heat tint) is observed.

1. Complete campaign change clean (Section 3.1) first.
2. Apply passivation paste (ammonium bifluoride-based) per manufacturer instructions to welds and heat-affected zones.
3. Dwell time: 20–30 min (do not let paste dry).
4. Rinse thoroughly with DM water.
5. Neutralise with dilute ammonia (1% solution) spray.
6. Final rinse with DM water until rinse pH 6.5–8.5.
7. Verify passivation with free-iron test kit (ASTM A380): blue colour = iron present → re-treat.

### 3.3 Pre-Commissioning / Post-Maintenance Cleaning

1. Inspect vessel interior for debris, tools, weld spatter.
2. Vacuum or wipe out any debris.
3. Follow campaign change clean (3.1) entirely.
4. If new welds were made, also complete Section 3.2 (Passivation).
5. Fill with DM water, hold 24 hr, drain, verify no leaks and water is clear.

| Item | Pass/Fail | Notes |
|---|---|---|

## 5. Frequency Summary

| Type | Frequency |
|---|---|
| Campaign change clean | Between different base types |
| Passivation | Quarterly or when discolouration seen |
| Pre-commissioning | Before first use, after welding/maintenance |
| Internal inspection | Monthly (visual) |
| Gasket replacement | Annually or when leaking |

---

## 6. Waste Handling

- All rinsate to process wastewater holding tank.
- Acid/alkali rinsate: neutralise to pH 6.5–8.5 before discharge to ETP (effluent treatment plant). Verify with pH strip.
- Passivation paste waste: collect in HDPE container, label as HAZ-WASTE-PAS-01, dispose through licensed contractor.
- Record volumes and pH in Environmental Log (PAL-ENV-001).
