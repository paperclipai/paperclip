# Cleaning SOP — High-Speed Disperser

**Document:** PAL-SOP-CLN-001
**Issue:** [PAL-39](/PAL/issues/PAL-39)
**Revision:** 1.0
**Date:** 2026-05-28

---

## 1. Purpose

To define the cleaning procedure for the high-speed disperser after each batch, between colour changes, and during extended shutdowns. Proper cleaning prevents cross-contamination between batches and maintains impeller balance and shaft seal integrity.

---

## 2. Equipment

| Item | Description |
|---|---|
| Disperser | Variable-speed, 30–50 HP, 300 mm impeller |
| Vessel | 500 L SS304 grind vessel |

---

## 3. Materials & Tools

| Material | Quantity per clean |
|---|---|
| DM water (hot, 50–60°C) | 50–80 L |
| Mild detergent (neutral pH, 7–8) | 200 mL |
| Clean rags / lint-free wipes | 5–10 |
| Plastic scraper (non-marring) | 1 |
| High-pressure wash gun (optional) | 1 |

**PPE required:** Chemical-resistant gloves, safety goggles, rubber apron, steel-toe boots.

---

## 4. Procedure

### 4.1 End-of-Batch Cleaning (Standard)

**Step 1 — Residual paint removal**
1. Ensure disperser is OFF and locked out (LOTO tag applied).
2. Lower impeller shaft fully into vessel.
3. Scrape residual paint from impeller blades and shaft using plastic scraper.
4. Collect scrapings in waste container. Do not flush bulk paint down drain.

**Step 2 — First rinse**
1. Add 30 L hot DM water (50–60°C) to vessel.
2. Raise impeller to 100 mm above vessel bottom.
3. Run disperser at 500 RPM for 5 min.
4. Drain rinse water into waste holding tank.
5. Inspect drained water — if heavily coloured, repeat step 2.

**Step 3 — Detergent wash**
1. Add 40 L hot DM water + 200 mL neutral detergent.
2. Run disperser at 800 RPM for 10 min.
3. Drain detergent solution into waste holding tank.

**Step 4 — Final rinse**
1. Add 30 L fresh hot DM water.
2. Run at 500 RPM for 5 min.
3. Drain completely.

**Step 5 — Visual inspection**
1. Inspect impeller blades, shaft, and vessel walls.
2. Pass criteria: No visible residue, no colour tint, water sheeting evenly (no beading indicates grease/oil residue).
3. If residue visible, repeat from Step 2.

### 4.2 Between Colour Change (Deep → Light)

Same as standard procedure (4.1) but repeat Steps 2–4 twice (2× rinse, 2× detergent, 2× final rinse).

### 4.3 Weekly Deep Clean

1. Follow standard procedure (4.1) Steps 1–5.
2. After Step 5, inspect impeller shaft seal for paint build-up. Clean with scraper and rag.
3. Remove impeller (if quick-release fitted), inspect both faces for dried paint, clean with wire brush (stainless) if needed.
4. Lubricate shaft seal with food-grade silicone grease (if applicable per manufacturer spec).
5. Re-install impeller, torque to manufacturer spec.

### 4.4 Extended Shutdown (>72 hr)

1. Complete standard clean (4.1).
2. Leave vessel lid open and shaft raised to allow air circulation.
3. Apply light rust-preventive oil to exposed shaft (food-grade, removable).

---

## 5. Verification

| Check | Method | Pass Criteria |
|---|---|---|
| Visual | White glove / clean rag wipe of impeller and vessel wall | No visible residue, rag comes away clean |
| Rinse water clarity | Collect 500 mL in clear glass beaker | Clear to very faint tint only |
| pH of final rinse | pH strip | 6.5–8.5 (same as feed water) |

---

## 6. Frequency

| Type | When |
|---|---|
| Standard | After every batch |
| Between colours | After each colour change |
| Deep clean | Weekly |
| Shutdown | Before shutdown >72 hr |

---

## 7. Waste Handling

- All rinse water and detergent solution must go to the **process wastewater holding tank** — never to storm drain.
- Solid paint scrapings go to **hazardous waste drum** (labelled HAZ-WASTE-01).
- Record volume discharged in Environmental Log (PAL-ENV-001).
