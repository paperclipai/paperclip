# Cleaning SOP — Transfer Pump

**Document:** PAL-SOP-CLN-004
**Issue:** [PAL-39](/PAL/issues/PAL-39)
**Revision:** 1.0
**Date:** 2026-05-28

**Applies to:**
- Pump P-101: Diaphragm / progressive cavity pump (grind → letdown transfer)
- Pump P-102: Transfer pump (letdown → filling machine)

---

## 1. Purpose

To define the cleaning procedure for the transfer pumps between batches and after extended use. Proper cleaning prevents dried paint from fouling check valves, diaphragms, or stators, and eliminates cross-contamination.

---

## 2. Materials & Tools

| Material | Quantity per clean |
|---|---|
| DM water (hot, 50–60°C) | 20–40 L |
| Neutral detergent (pH 7–8) | 100 mL |
| Solvent flush (mineral turpentine or xylene) | 2 L (stubborn residue only) |
| Clean rags / lint-free wipes | 5 |
| Soft brush (nylon) | 1 |
| Bucket (clean, 20 L) | 2 |
| Hose adapter (for recirculation loop) | 1 |

**PPE required:** Chemical-resistant gloves, safety goggles, rubber apron. If using solvent: nitrile gloves, respirator (organic vapour cartridge).

---

## 3. Procedure

### 3.1 Standard End-of-Batch Cleaning

**Step 1 — Flush with water**
1. Ensure pump is OFF and LOTO applied. Close inlet and outlet valves.
2. Disconnect inlet hose from vessel. Place in bucket of clean hot DM water.
3. Disconnect outlet hose, direct to wastewater drain or bucket.
4. Re-energize pump (LOTO removed, supervisor authorisation).
5. Run pump at minimum speed until exiting water runs clear (typically 2–5 min).
6. Stop pump.

**Step 2 — Detergent recirculation**
1. Prepare 20 L hot DM water + 100 mL neutral detergent in clean bucket.
2. Place inlet hose in bucket. Run pump at slow speed.
3. Recirculate detergent through pump for 10 min (outlet hose back into the same bucket).
4. Drain detergent. Dispose to wastewater.

**Step 3 — Final flush**
1. Place inlet hose in bucket of clean hot DM water.
2. Run pump until exiting water is clear (no foam, no colour).
3. Stop pump.
4. Disconnect hoses. Allow pump to drain fully.

**Step 4 — External wipe-down**
1. Wipe pump exterior with damp rag.
2. Inspect for leaks around seals, gaskets, and pipe connections.
3. Report any drips or weeps to maintenance.

### 3.2 Solvent Flush (Heavy Residue Only)

If water flushing does not clear blockage or coloured residue persists:
1. Complete standard clean (3.1) first.
2. Place inlet hose in 2 L mineral turpentine or xylene.
3. Run pump at low speed for 30 seconds (do NOT recirculate — discharge to labelled solvent waste container).
4. Immediately follow with standard flush (3.1, Steps 3–4) to remove solvent residues.
5. Note: Solvent flush is for progressive cavity pumps only. Do NOT use solvent on diaphragm pumps unless manufacturer approved.

### 3.3 Weekly / Monthly Deep Clean

| Frequency | Task |
|---|---|
| Weekly | Remove inlet strainer/filter. Clean with detergent and brush. Inspect for holes or damage. |
| Monthly | Inspect diaphragm (diaphragm pump) or stator (PC pump) for wear. Disassemble per manufacturer manual, clean all internal surfaces. Replace worn parts. |

---

## 4. Verification

| Check | Method | Pass Criteria |
|---|---|---|
| Flush water clarity | Visual in clear container | Clear, no colour |
| Flow rate | Measure L/min with bucket + stopwatch | Within 10% of rated flow (50 LPM target) |
| External leak check | Visual during flush | No drips at seals or connections |
| Strainer cleanliness | Visual | No debris, no holes |

---

## 5. Frequency

| Type | When |
|---|---|
| Standard flush | After every batch transfer |
| Solvent flush | Only when water fails to clear residue |
| Strainer clean | Weekly |
| Internal inspection | Monthly |

---

## 6. Waste Handling

- All water/detergent rinsate to process wastewater holding tank.
- Solvent flush waste: collect in labelled HDPE container → hazardous waste drum. Do NOT mix with water waste.
- Record solvent volume in Environmental Log (PAL-ENV-001).
