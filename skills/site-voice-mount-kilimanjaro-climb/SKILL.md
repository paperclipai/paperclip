# Site Voice — Mount Kilimanjaro Climb

## Brand in one line

> The dedicated Kilimanjaro operator. Data on every route. 96% summit rate this season. Safety procedures that aren't marketing fluff.

## Who we are

- **Pure Kili operator** — we don't lead with safaris (leave that to sister site Safari Kilimanjaro). Kili is the product.
- **Six routes** — Machame, Marangu, Rongai, Lemosho, Umbwe, Northern Circuit. We know each one.
- **Data-confident** — published success rates by route, acclimatization protocols, summit weather patterns.
- **Safety-first but not scared** — we publish evacuation rates, AMS incidence, what to expect. Transparent, not alarming.
- **SuccessRateCalculator** — our interactive tool that predicts your summit probability by route + day count + fitness. It's our flagship engagement asset.

## Voice anchors

| Trait | Yes | No |
|---|---|---|
| Tone | Confident-athletic, matter-of-fact | Hype or corporate |
| Posture | "Here's the data. Make your decision." | "Dream big. We'll take care of it." |
| Rhythm | Short, declarative. Numbers. Verbs. | Flowery. Adjective-heavy. |
| Expertise | Shown through specificity (altitudes, gradients, climb days) | Claimed with "expert" + "experienced" |
| Safety | Concrete protocols | Generic "highest standards" |

## Vocabulary

### Use
- Altitude: "5,895 m", "4,700 m Kibo summit approach", "3,720 m Shira Plateau"
- Route names: "Machame 7-day", "Lemosho 8-day", "Umbwe 6-day" — always include day count
- Metrics: "summit rate", "AMS incidence", "evacuation rate"
- "Acclimatization day", "pole pole" (Swahili for "slowly" — Kili tradition)
- "Porter", "lead guide", "assistant guide" — crew roles named specifically
- "Summit attempt", "summit push", "Uhuru Peak"
- "Stella Point" (5,756 m — the ridge before Uhuru)
- "Barranco Wall", "Kissing Rock", "Western Breach" — named landmarks

### Avoid
- "Roof of Africa" — overused in marketing; OK in H1 ONCE per site, not in body.
- "Adventure", "journey", "experience" — low-signal.
- "Conquer the mountain" — we respect the mountain, we don't conquer it.
- "Best", "world-class", "highest standards" — replace with specific numbers.
- "Easy" or "difficult" without context — every route has pros and cons at different fitness levels.
- Toxic competitive: "vs Altezza" (can't name) — just own the data.

### Forbidden (hook v12 P9 + P5)
- Altezza Travel, Tanzania Specialist, Peak Planet, Climb Kilimanjaro Guide, Bobby Camping, Micato, Nomad, andBeyond — never named.
- **mountkilimanjaro.com** — hook v12 P14 rejects this. The only our-domain is `mountkilimanjaroclimb.com` (with "climb"). Don't link or reference `mountkilimanjaro.com` in any file.

## Paragraph feel (example)

### ✅ On-brand
> The Lemosho route crosses the mountain from west to east over 8 days. You start at 2,100 m in rainforest, sleep at 2,750 m on day 1, and climb to 3,500 m on day 2. By day 4 you're at 4,100 m — that altitude drop back to Barranco Camp at 3,960 m is what gives Lemosho its summit rate advantage. You acclimatize on the way up. By summit night you've slept above 4,000 m for three nights. That's why 88% of our Lemosho climbers reach Uhuru Peak. On the shorter Marangu 5-day, the number drops to 45%. Route choice matters more than fitness.

### ❌ Off-brand
> Embark on the ultimate adventure and conquer the roof of Africa with our expert team! Our experienced guides have helped countless climbers reach the summit of majestic Kilimanjaro. With world-class service and the highest safety standards, we'll make your dream climb unforgettable!

## OK to mention / Don't mention

| OK | Don't |
|---|---|
| "88% summit success on Lemosho 8-day" | "High success rate" |
| "KPAP partner — porters fed 3 meals/day, proper gear, correct wages" | "Ethical porter treatment" (vague) |
| "HAPE incidence: 0.2% on our 8-day routes" | Glossing over altitude risk |
| "From $2,400 all-in for Lemosho 8-day" | Hidden pricing |
| "We don't run the 5-day. It's not safe enough." | Promoting Marangu 5-day just because it's cheap |

## Safety language rules

- **Always publish** route-specific success rates. Never fudge numbers.
- **Always describe** acclimatization profile (sleep altitudes + climb altitudes per day).
- **Always mention** KPAP partner status (for ethical porter wages/gear).
- **Always include** oxygen cylinder + pulse ox + emergency evacuation info on FAQ.
- **Never hype** — "once-in-a-lifetime experience" — factual substitute: "5,895 m, Africa's highest point, 4 climate zones in 7 days".
