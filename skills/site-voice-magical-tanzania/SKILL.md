# Site Voice — Magical Tanzania

## Brand in one line

> The thinking traveler's Tanzania guide. Editorial, opinionated, transparent. We recommend camps the way a good magazine recommends restaurants.

## Who we are

- **Editorial brand** — the copy reads like a premium travel magazine (Condé Nast, Travel+Leisure), not a booking engine.
- **Unbiased** — we recommend the best camp for YOUR trip, not the one paying us the highest commission.
- **Transparent pricing** — every itinerary page shows a "from $X,XXX per person" number. We never hide behind "contact for quote".
- **529 pages, 10 languages** — big library. Scale demands editorial discipline.
- **Sister sites** — Safari Kilimanjaro (Kili combos), Safaris Tanzania (sister operator). Cross-link but don't confuse the user.

## Voice anchors

| Trait | Yes | No |
|---|---|---|
| Tone | Editorial-warm, essayistic | Sales copy, aspirational |
| Posture | "Here's what we think" (opinion with reasoning) | "Here's what we sell" |
| Structure | Lede → context → insider angle → transparent CTA | Problem → solution → urgency |
| Evidence | Specific camps, specific dates, specific prices, specific guide names | Generic superlatives |
| Reader | Informed, curious, sophisticated, probably not first-time safari-goer | First-time buyer in panic-research mode |

## Vocabulary

### Use
- "We recommend", "we think", "in our view", "our travellers most often choose" — opinion-forward
- "From $X,XXX per person" — always visible pricing
- "Shoulder season", "migration calendar", "mobile camps" — vocabulary of the informed traveller
- "Unbiased", "transparent", "no hidden fees"
- Camp names specifically — "Serengeti Under Canvas", "Asanja Africa", "Serian's Serengeti Mobile" (these ARE allowed; they're CAMPS, not operator competitors, though double-check hook P9)
- Named guides when praising: "Our guide Francis has 18 years…"

### Avoid
- "Adventure", "bucket list", "dream destination", "breathtaking" — all trite.
- "Contact for quote", "tailored to your budget" when not paired with a real number.
- "Best" without evidence. Prefer "our favourite for X reason".
- Corporate "we" without specificity. Who is "we"? The editorial team.
- British vs American spelling — pick ONE and stay consistent per locale. English = British (magical is British-leaning), US for US audience.

### Forbidden (hook v12 P9)
- **Operator competitors**: Micato, Altezza, Tanzania Specialist, Nomad Tanzania, Bobby Camping, Karibu Kilifair, andBeyond — never named.
- **Camp brands are allowed** (Singita, Asilia, Four Seasons) ONLY when reviewing them neutrally and NOT in a way that positions them as "our camps" (we don't own them). Double-check hook v12 — it lists some of these.

## Sentence patterns

### Openings
- "October in the southern Serengeti is a quieter month than the calendar suggests."
- "If you asked us to name the three most underrated camps in Tanzania, we'd start with Kuro."
- "Most travellers come to Ngorongoro for two nights. We think one is enough."

### Transitions
- "Here's what we'd do differently:"
- "The reason we hesitate to recommend this:"
- "Where this gets interesting:"

### Closings
- "Talk to our travel experts" (primary CTA)
- "See the full itinerary" (for related page handoff)
- "Start planning your trip"

## Paragraph feel (example)

### ✅ On-brand
> Kuro Tented Camp sits on a thin strip of mopane forest above the Tarangire floodplain. From your tent deck — canvas walls, iron bedframe, bucket shower heated by the staff at 5pm — you watch a giraffe graze twenty metres away for an hour without moving. That's the experience. It's not a Four Seasons. It's better: it's a place where the camp disappears and the country remains. From $950 per person per night, full board. We'd put three nights here in any migration-season itinerary.

### ❌ Off-brand
> Experience the ultimate Tanzania safari at our carefully selected luxury camps! Wake up to breathtaking views and immerse yourself in an unforgettable adventure. Our travel experts will customize your perfect African getaway. Contact us today for a personalized quote!

## Meta description template

```
[Specific insight or pick]. [Price or timing specificity]. [Editorial CTA].
```

Example:
> "Our three favourite camps in southern Serengeti, with October pricing from $950pp/night. We'll tell you which to pick for migration timing. See our picks." (159 chars)

## OK to mention / Don't mention

| OK | Don't |
|---|---|
| "Serengeti Under Canvas, June 2026 pricing" | "Exclusive luxury accommodations" |
| "Our favourite mobile camp for the migration" | "Best mobile camp in Africa" |
| "We don't rate Ngorongoro Serena Lodge for the money" (honest negative opinion) | Positive-only fake review vibe |
| "From $4,200 per person, 7-day private" | "Call for pricing" |

## Pricing transparency rule

EVERY itinerary page and destination page MUST show a "from $X,XXX per person" visible number above the fold.

If pricing isn't known: add `TouristTrip.offers.priceRange` schema and write "Pricing depends on camp and season — from $X–$Y per person. We'll quote exactly after a short call." (Still honest; still transparent about the range.)

Reviewer: **reject PRs** that put "contact for quote" as the ONLY pricing information.

## Locale notes

- 10 locales. Editorial voice translates but needs careful per-locale briefing. Japanese/Korean may soften "we don't rate X" slightly (cultural directness norms).
- Never auto-translate editorial copy. It will sound like marketing copy after MT.
