# Site Voice — Safaris Tanzania

## Brand in one line

> A direct-operator Tanzania safari company for travelers who've done their research and want someone honest, not a salesperson.

## Who we are

- **Direct operator** — own the vehicles, employ the guides. Not a broker, not a reseller, not a concierge service.
- **11 locales** — ar, de, en, es, fr, hi, it, ja, ko, nl, pt, zh. Global audience.
- **Competitor context** — tanzania-specialist.com (Altezza Travel) dominates this query cluster with heavy budget. We win on honesty, depth, and direct-contact.
- **Framer Motion** aesthetic — elegant animations on key pages. Visual design signals craft.

## Brand colors + fonts

- **Primary**: amber `#D4A017` (warm, grounded)
- **Secondary**: forest green `#4A5A2B` (natural, not corporate)
- **Typography**: Cormorant Garamond (display), Source Serif 4 (body long-form), DM Sans (UI)

Don't describe these in copy, but know them so copy matches visual weight.

## Voice anchors

| Trait | Yes | No |
|---|---|---|
| Tone | Warm-confident, direct | Aspirational, hype |
| Posture | "Here's what you'll actually experience" | "The adventure of a lifetime awaits" |
| Authority | Earned through specificity | Claimed with superlatives |
| Urgency | Only when real (migration timing, park closures) | Fabricated scarcity |
| Emotion | Sense of place ("Tarangire at dusk smells like elephant dung and baobab resin") | Sensory clichés ("majestic", "breathtaking") |
| Formality | Informed-casual, expert friend | Stuffy, PR-speak |

## Vocabulary

### Use
- "Direct operator", "our guides", "our vehicles", "our camp partners" (we own or have real contracts — not "preferred partners")
- "Migration", "Serengeti", "Tarangire", "Ngorongoro", "Ruaha" (deep destination knowledge)
- "Green season" / "short rains" / "long rains" (dates are code for savvy)
- "Private", "customized" (accurate claims)
- "Pick-up in Arusha" (concrete, not "seamless logistics")
- Specifics: "Toyota Land Cruiser 70-series", "camp capacity 12 guests max", "guides certified KPSGA Silver"

### Avoid
- "Bucket list", "dream destination", "once-in-a-lifetime"
- "Our team", "our experts" (vague; use job titles or names)
- "Affordable luxury" (incoherent)
- "Hidden gem" (if it's hidden, why is there a page about it)
- Excessive exclamation marks

### Forbidden (hook v12 P9)
- Tanzania Specialist, Micato, Altezza, Nomad Tanzania, Bobby Camping, Karibu Kilifair, Singita, Asilia, Four Seasons, andBeyond — never mention.

## Sentence patterns

### Openings
- "Most Tanzania safaris cost too much because they pass through three middlemen. We are the first one."
- "If you've narrowed your research to [destination], here's what you'll actually see in September…"
- "We're not going to call this the Big Five experience of a lifetime. Here's what it actually is:"

### Transitions
- "Where this falls apart for most operators:"
- "Here's the part most travel agents won't tell you:"
- "What you'll actually feel:"

### Closings (CTA)
- "Plan your safari" (primary CTA)
- "Let's design your trip together"
- "Talk to our guides" (when CTA is for expert call)

## Paragraph feel (example)

### ✅ On-brand
> A good guide is the difference between a safari and a drive-through. Ours have worked the northern circuit for an average of 12 years. They know which Tarangire bulls come to the same kopje every dry season, which Ngorongoro lion pride has cubs this year, which lodges will actually honor a late dinner request. That knowledge isn't in a guidebook.

### ❌ Off-brand
> Our expert team of safari specialists will craft the perfect luxury Tanzania safari experience for you! From majestic wildlife to breathtaking landscapes, every moment is designed to exceed your expectations. Book your dream African adventure today!

## CTA verbs

1. **Plan your safari** — primary
2. **Start a conversation**
3. **Design your trip**
4. **Talk to our guides**

Avoid: "Book now", "Reserve today", "Click here"

## Meta description template

```
[Direct-operator differentiator or specific]. [Concrete detail]. [Planning CTA].
```

Example:
> "Direct-operator Tanzania safaris with our own vehicles and Arusha-based guides. Customized itineraries, 12-year-average guide experience. Plan your trip." (160 chars)

## OK to mention / Don't mention

| OK | Don't |
|---|---|
| "Our vehicles: 12 Toyota Land Cruisers" | "Premium fleet" |
| "KPSGA Silver certified guides" | "Expert guides" |
| "Arusha-based, family-run since 2008" | "Award-winning" (unless we have a specific named award) |
| "From $350/person/day" | Hidden pricing with "contact for quote" everywhere |
| "July-October is peak migration in Serengeti North" | "Anytime is safari season" |

## Locale notes

- 11 locales. Mid-tier complexity.
- Voice is direct + warm across all. Adjust formality per culture (Japanese more formal, German retains "du" for travel context).
- Don't auto-translate marketing copy. Hand-brief per locale.
