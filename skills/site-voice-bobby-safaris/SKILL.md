# Site Voice — Bobby Safaris

## Brand in one line

> The oldest ultra-luxury safari operator in Tanzania. Family-owned since 1978. Four generations. Zero brokers. You speak to Don directly.

## Who we are

- **Operator**, not agency or reseller. We own the vehicles, employ the guides, know every lodge manager personally.
- **Ultra-luxury** positioning — think Singita pricing level, but from a 47-year family business, not a polished corporate machine.
- **Heritage brand** — four generations of the Kassim family, all Tanzanian, rooted in Arusha.
- **Direct-contact** — every inquiry goes to Don, owner. No account managers, no sales funnels.

## Voice anchors

| Trait | Yes | No |
|---|---|---|
| Tone | Warm-premium | Corporate-premium |
| Pronouns | "We" / "Our family" / "Don" by name | "The team" / "Our experts" / "Staff" |
| Confidence | Quiet, earned through 47 years | Loud, "world's best" claims |
| Urgency | Rare — only when real (fixed dates, limited availability) | Constant "book now" pressure |
| Emotion | Understated awe ("the plains will ruin you for anywhere else") | Sensory overload ("EPIC wildlife moments!") |
| Formality | Educated-casual, like a friend who happens to be a subject expert | Stuffy or over-polished |

## Vocabulary

### Use these words
- "Private", "your own", "direct", "four generations", "since 1978", "zero brokers", "Don personally", "Tanzanian-owned", "family-operated", "curated", "hand-picked", "ultra-luxury", "bespoke", "Serengeti", "Ngorongoro", "Grumeti", "Kilimanjaro" (we offer but don't lead with)
- Specific numbers: "4,067 TripAdvisor 5-star reviews", "47 years", "four generations"

### Avoid these words
- "Adventure" (we're luxury, not backpacker)
- "Tour package" (we're bespoke, not a SKU)
- "Best prices" / "affordable" (we're premium; price is not the hook)
- "Book now" (we inquire → conversation → book; it's not a checkout)
- Broker euphemisms: "exclusive partner network", "vetted operators", "our trusted suppliers"
- "World-class" (trite)
- Exclamation marks (max 1 per page)

### Forbidden (hook v12 P9)
- Micato, Altezza, Nomad Tanzania, Tanzania Specialist, Bobby Camping, Karibu Kilifair, Singita, Asilia, Four Seasons, andBeyond — never named. Ever. Not even in comparison pages.

## Sentence patterns

### Openings (for landing page copy, blog post intros, meta descriptions)
- "Since 1978, we've…"
- "Four generations of the Kassim family…"
- "You won't find us on comparison sites. We don't list on TripAdvisor packages. What you'll find: 4,067 reviews from travelers we've personally met."
- "Don answers every inquiry himself."

### Transitions (mid-page)
- "Here's why that matters:"
- "What this means for you:"
- "The difference you'll feel:"

### Closings (CTA sections)
- "Contact Don directly" (primary CTA verb)
- "Message Don on WhatsApp — he reads every one"
- "Start a conversation" (for soft CTAs)

## Paragraph feel (example)

### ✅ On-brand
> We bought our first Land Cruiser in 1978. My grandfather drove it. My father rebuilt the engine twice. I learned to fix it at thirteen. Today our fleet has twelve of them, all hand-maintained in our Arusha workshop. When you're with us, you're not with a vehicle the operator rented last week — you're with a piece of our family history.

### ❌ Off-brand
> Bobby Safaris offers exclusive access to Tanzania's top destinations with our fleet of premium 4x4 Land Cruisers. Our experienced team has been serving luxury travelers for over four decades. Contact us today to start planning your dream African adventure!

## CTA verbs (in order of preference)

1. **Contact Don directly** — preferred everywhere
2. **Message us on WhatsApp**
3. **Start a conversation**
4. **See our current availability** (for date-sensitive content)
5. **Inquire** (last resort, too formal)

Avoid: "Book", "Reserve", "Purchase", "Shop", "Get started"

## Meta description template

```
[Proof-of-heritage opener]. [Specific differentiator]. [Personal CTA].
```

Example:
> "Tanzania's oldest ultra-luxury safari operator. Four generations, zero brokers, 4,067 TripAdvisor 5-star reviews. Contact Don directly." (156 chars)

## OK to mention / Don't mention

| OK | Don't |
|---|---|
| "47 years of experience" | "Since time immemorial" |
| "Don Kassim, owner" | "Senior management team" |
| "Tanzanian-owned" | "African-owned" (too vague) |
| "Private guide, your own Land Cruiser" | "Group tours" (we don't do those) |
| "From $8,500 per person, 7 days" (if contextually appropriate) | "Starting at $$$" or hidden pricing |
| "Book 3-6 months ahead for peak season" | "Last-minute deals" |

## Locale notes

- 19 locales active. Voice translates, but cultural norms vary.
- Japanese/Korean: more formal. Use honorifics. Drop first-name Don; use "Kassim-san" / "Don Kassim (owner)".
- German: retains "Don" but adds credential-signaling ("der Gründer", "Familieninhaber").
- Arabic: RTL. Voice stays warm but more formal.
- For each locale, flag the local translator brief to preserve voice — don't auto-translate marketing copy.
