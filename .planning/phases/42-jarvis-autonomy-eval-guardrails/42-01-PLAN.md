# Phase 42 Plan 01: Jarvis Autonomy Eval Guardrails

<objective>
Constrain Jarvis knowledge rewrites to proposal-only, eval-backed, approval-visible workflows with deterministic fallback evaluation and operations monitoring.
</objective>

<must_haves>
## Requirements
- AUTO-01: Jarvis rewrite output includes proposed diff, evidence, risk, and approval route, with no direct apply path.
- AUTO-02: Provider-backed eval and deterministic fallback eval share one rubric schema.
- AUTO-03: Evaluation comparison and production monitoring expose provider unavailable, disagreement, low-confidence, grounding, citation freshness, contradiction warning, and rewrite proposal quality states.

## Decisions Covered
- D-01 through D-04: proposal-only rewrite boundary and no direct apply path.
- D-05 through D-08: shared eval rubric with provider optionality and deterministic fallback.
- D-09 through D-12: approval/activity/contradiction linkage and company boundary.
- D-13 through D-15: dense monitoring in existing operations/Jarvis/governance surfaces.
- D-16 through D-18: deterministic route/service/UI test coverage without live providers.
</must_haves>

<implementation_steps>
1. Add shared Jarvis rewrite proposal, eval rubric, eval comparison, and monitoring summary contracts.
2. Add company-scoped persistence for rewrite proposals and eval runs with citation IDs, contradiction IDs, provider/fallback statuses, risk, approval ID, and activity evidence.
3. Extend `rt2JarvisService` with proposal creation, deterministic fallback rubric evaluation, optional provider comparison input, approval request creation, proposal decision recording, and proposal listing.
4. Add Jarvis proposal/eval routes under existing company-scoped Jarvis routing. Do not add a direct apply route.
5. Extend knowledge operations health with rewrite proposal monitoring, reason codes for provider unavailable/disagreement/low confidence/blocked proposals, and flow links.
6. Extend the existing Jarvis quality UI/API surface with recent rewrite proposals and eval/approval evidence.
7. Add deterministic tests for proposal creation, no direct apply route, provider unavailable fallback, disagreement, low confidence, approval linkage, contradiction linkage, and monitoring reason codes.
</implementation_steps>

<threat_model>
## Assets
- Company-scoped RT2 knowledge, wiki, graph, contradiction, approval, and activity evidence.
- Jarvis rewrite proposals and eval outputs that could influence operator decisions.

## Threats
- High: Direct rewrite apply bypasses human approval and silently mutates canonical knowledge.
- High: Provider failure or nondeterministic eval is hidden as success.
- Medium: Cross-company proposal/eval data leaks through routes or monitoring.
- Medium: Low-confidence or contradiction-backed proposals look safe in UI.

## Mitigations
- No direct apply route/service is introduced; proposal decisions only record approval/rejection evidence.
- Provider status, fallback status, disagreement, confidence, citation freshness, and contradiction IDs are persisted and surfaced.
- Every route uses `assertCompanyAccess`; every stored row includes `companyId`.
- Tests cover no direct apply, provider unavailable, disagreement, low confidence, contradiction linkage, and monitoring reasons.
</threat_model>
