# 5 Metrics Every Ops Director Should Track in Their Briefing Process

**By CrewBrief Operations** · May 2026 · 6 min read

---

What gets measured gets managed. In flight operations, the briefing process produces a wealth of data that most operators never capture. Here are the five metrics that separate a managed briefing program from one that runs on hope.

## 1. Briefing Completion Rate

**What it is:** The percentage of scheduled flight duties where a briefing was actually viewed before departure.

**Why it matters:** If crews are skipping briefings, the SMS program has a gap. A completion rate below 90% means some crews are launching without a complete picture of the day's risks.

**Target:** 95%+.

**How to improve:** Make briefings faster to access (one tap vs. five apps) and harder to skip (readiness gate requires completion).

## 2. FRAT Override Rate

**What it is:** The percentage of FRAT assessments where the pilot changed at least one pre-filled risk factor.

**Why it matters:** A 0% override rate means either the automation is perfect or crews are blindly accepting scores. A healthy override rate (5–15%) indicates crews are engaged and applying their judgment to the factors that require it.

**Target:** 5–15%.

**How to improve:** If overrides cluster around specific factors (e.g., fatigue scoring), investigate whether the automation is scoring correctly or if crews need more guidance.

## 3. Time to Brief

**What it is:** The average time from opening the briefing to completing the readiness acknowledgment.

**Why it matters:** Time is the most visible measure of efficiency. Six minutes is fast. Eighteen minutes is fragmented. Thirty minutes means your process needs a redesign.

**Target:** Under 8 minutes per shift.

**How to improve:** Fewer data sources, pre-filled FRAT, role-optimized views that surface the most relevant information first.

## 4. Delta Review Lag

**What it is:** The time between a significant data change (SIGMET, runway closure, amended release) and the crew's acknowledgment of that change.

**Why it matters:** Thirty minutes without acknowledgment means thirty minutes of operating on stale information. In rapidly changing weather or NOTAM conditions, that gap is a risk exposure.

**Target:** Under 15 minutes for critical changes.

**How to improve:** Push notifications for high-severity changes, in-briefing delta highlights, escalation to ops if unacknowledged beyond threshold.

## 5. Issue Recurrence Rate

**What it is:** The percentage of briefing errors, missed items, or data gaps that recur after being identified and addressed.

**Why it matters:** Recurrence reveals systemic problems. A data source that consistently fails, a warning threshold that produces false positives, a briefing format that buries critical information — these are not one-time bugs.

**Target:** Under 10% month-over-month.

**How to improve:** Log every identified issue with root cause. Review the log weekly. Fix systemic issues, not individual complaints.

---

## Building the Dashboard

These five metrics form the core of a briefing operations dashboard. Track them weekly, share with the ops team monthly, and review trends quarterly.

An automated briefing system should surface all five metrics without manual data collection. If yours doesn't, you're flying blind.
